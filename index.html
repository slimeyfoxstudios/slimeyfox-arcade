Slimey Blaster – Game Design Document

1. Game Overview

Concept: Slimey Blaster is a fast-paced arcade shooter where players control a slime-shooting hero navigating colorful, hazardous environments. Dodge obstacles, defeat slime-mutant enemies, and collect power-ups to survive escalating waves of challenges.

Core Gameplay Loop:

  • Enter level → navigate terrain → avoid hazards
  • Shoot slimes → earn points → collect power-ups
  • Defeat mini-bosses → reach level exit
  • Progressively harder levels with more enemies and environmental hazards

2. Gameplay Mechanics

Player Controls

  • Move: Arrow keys / WASD / Virtual joystick
  • Shoot: Space / Tap button
  • Dash / Boost: Shift / Swipe
  • Special Ability: E / Gesture

Combat Mechanics

  • Slime Blasts: Standard attack, can hit multiple enemies if charged
  • Power Slimes: Drop power-ups or points
  • Boss Encounters: Mini-bosses with unique patterns per level

Scoring System

  • Points per enemy destroyed
  • Combo multiplier for consecutive hits without damage
  • Bonus points for speedrun completion

Player Progression

  • Unlock new slime types (fire slime, ice slime)
  • Upgrade attack speed, damage, dash cooldown with coins
  • Optional cosmetic skins

3. Levels & Environments

Level Themes

  • Slime Swamp: sticky traps, green goo, small slime mobs
  • Crystal Caverns: reflective crystals, narrow pathways, bouncing slimes
  • Volcanic Core: lava pits, fire slimes, fast hazards
  • Neon City: futuristic, neon platforms, electric slime hazards

Environmental Hazards

  • Spike traps
  • Rolling boulders / debris
  • Slime puddles slow movement
  • Lava / fire zones

Level Objectives

  • Reach the exit point
  • Optional side objectives: collect coins, destroy all enemies, complete in record time

4. Enemies & Bosses

Regular Enemies

  • Slime Minions – basic, slow, numerous
  • Spiky Slimes – small, bounce unpredictably
  • Exploding Slimes – detonate on proximity
  • Flying Slimes – attack from above

Bosses

  • Slime King – shoots projectiles, splits into smaller slimes
  • Crystal Slime – reflective shield, timing-based weak points
  • Lava Golem – fire-based heavy attacks with knockback
  • Neon Phantom – fast, teleporting, predictive attacks

5. Art & Visual Style

  • Cartoonish, vivid 2D/2.5D style
  • Bright, saturated colors per level
  • Squishy slime physics, bouncy movement, fluid attacks
  • Particle effects: slime splashes, glowing projectiles, screen shake

6. Audio Design

  • Music: Arcade-style synthwave / chiptune, dynamic tracks per level intensity
  • Sound Effects: Slime squelches, explosions, power-up chimes, dash & special cues
  • Voice/UI Prompts: Fun optional voice clips, minimalistic HUD sounds

7. User Interface (UI)

  • HUD: Health, ammo/slime meter, score & combo multiplier, power-up icons
  • Menus: Start, Options, Leaderboard, Pause, Level selection
  • Settings: Audio, key/controller mapping, graphics quality

8. Monetization & Extras (Optional)

  • Cosmetic skins (non-pay-to-win)
  • Achievements & leaderboard
  • In-game currency to unlock slime types / upgrades
  • Optional ads or premium version

9. Technical Requirements

  • Engine: Unity / Unreal Engine 5
  • Platforms: PC, Console, Mobile (iOS & Android)
  • Performance: 60 FPS on mid-tier devices
  • Resolution: Scalable from 720p to 4K

10. Development Roadmap

  • Prototype: Player movement & shooting
  • Alpha: First two levels, basic enemies, scoring system
  • Beta: Full levels, bosses, upgrades, UI integration
  • Release Candidate: Polish, optimization, sound & effects
  • Launch: Marketing, store submission, post-launch updates
